Dashashwamedh Ghat is special because it is the most spectacular and lively ghat in Varanasi, famous for its daily Ganga Aarti ceremony, where priests offer prayers with lamps to the holy river. It is also one of the oldest and holiest ghats, steeped in legend, with a history of Lord Brahma performing the Dasa-Ashwamedha yajna here. The ghat offers a vibrant atmosphere, stunning views, and a deep connection to the spiritual and cultural essence of Varanasi.

üõï Temples

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is special because it houses one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, believed to be self-manifestations of Lord Shiva, making it a central spiritual destination for Hindus. Located in the holy city of Varanasi, on the banks of the Ganges, it's also renowned for its deep-rooted history, architectural beauty, and the powerful spiritual aura it holds. The recent Kashi Vishwanath Dham project has further enhanced accessibility and beautified the area, making it a prime symbol of cultural heritage and urban renewal.

üçõ Local Food

The special aspects of Banarasi Kachori Sabzi include its unique preparation with a thin, unleavened puri dough, often served in a leaf-made bowl (pattal). The accompanying sabzi, particularly the flavorful Raswale Aloo (thin, tangy potato curry), is light and spicy, balancing the greasiness of the fried kachoris. This traditional morning breakfast also often features **Dal ki Pithi (larger kachoris with lentil filling) or smaller hing-spice stuffed kachoris, sometimes served with black chickpeas (kala chana)
